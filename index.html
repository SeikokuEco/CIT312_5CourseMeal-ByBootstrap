Appetizer

Start your meal off with Shrimp Cocktail

Recipe - Summarized
    Shrimp
  1. Boil: 3 qt water + lemon + ½ onion + 2 bay leaves + sugar + salt
  2. Prep: Ice bath ready
  3. Cook: Shrimp in batches, 2-3 min until pink + floating
  4. Chill: Straight into ice bath, then drain + refrigerate
    Sauce
  1. Mix: Ketchup + lemon juice + lemon zest + horseradish + Tabasco + Worcestershire
  2. Chill until serving

Soup

Second Course is Clam Chowder Soup

Recipe - Summarized
  1. Crisp bacon; keep 3 Tbsp drippings
  2. Sauté carrots, onion, celery; add flour
  3. Add broth, clam juice, seasonings; bring to light boil
  4. Add potatoes, milk, cream; simmer until tender
  5. Stir in clams; brief boil; remove from heat
  6. Top with bacon (and cilantro if desired)

Fish

Dive into your third course with this Grilled Salmon and Vegetables

Recipe - Summarized
  1. Cut salmon into 6 portions; place skin-side down in a baking dish
  2. Whisk soy sauce, Dijon, garlic, and olive oil until emulsified
  3. Reserve 2 Tbsp marinade; spread the rest over salmon and rest 10 min
  4. Preheat grill to medium-high (about 400°F); clean and oil grates
  5. Grill salmon skin-side down ~4 min, flip, cook ~4 min more
  6. Remove skin, plate salmon, and brush with reserved marinade
  7. Garnish with parsley if desired

Meat

After Fish, feast on a classic Beef Wellington

Recipe - Summarized
    Beef
  1. Tie tenderloin with kitchen twine every 2 inches; season with salt and pepper
  2. Let rest on a rack at room temp for 1 hour
  3. Sear in canola oil until browned on all sides (10-12 min)
  4. Cool 15 min; remove twine
  5. Brush with Dijon; coat with porcini powder
  6. Chill uncovered 1-3 hours
    Duxelles
  1. Pulse mushrooms + shallots in batches until very finely chopped
  2. Cook mixture in butter until dry and browning (25-30 min)
  3. Add garlic + thyme; cook 1 min
  4. Add sherry + pepper; cook until dry again (2-4 min)
  5. Spread on sheet pan and chill until cold
    Assemble Wellington
  1. Lay overlapping plastic wrap to form a large square
  2. Overlap phyllo sheets; top with prosciutto, leaving small side borders
  3. Spread duxelles evenly; sprinkle with chives + parsley
  4. Place chilled beef on bottom third; roll tightly using plastic wrap
  5. Chill log while preparing puff pastry
    Wrap in Puff Pastry
  1. Roll puff pastry to a 15x12-inch rectangle
  2. Brush top third lightly with beaten egg
  3. Unwrap beef log; place on bottom edge and roll to seal
  4. Seal seam and fold ends under
  5. Transfer seam-side down to parchment-lined sheet
  6. Brush with egg wash; lightly score decorative pattern
  7. Cut 3 small steam vents; sprinkle with flaky salt
    Bake
  1. Bake at 425°F until pastry is browned and beef reaches 120°F (40-45 min)
  2. Rest 15 min before slicing
  3. Serve with thyme branches

Dessert

For Dessert, have yourself a lovely Crème Brûlée

Recipe - Summarized
  1. Preheat oven to 300°F
  2. Beat egg yolks and sugar until pale and sugar is dissolved
  3. Add whipping cream and vanilla; mix on low until blended
  4. Strain mixture if desired; divide into 4 ramekins
  5. Place ramekins in a baking dish and add hot water halfway up the sides
  6. Bake 45-55 minutes until custard is set
  7. Remove from water bath; cool on rack, then chill at least 2 hours
  8. Before serving, sprinkle ~1½ tsp sugar on each custard
  9. Caramelize sugar with a torch or broil 4-5 minutes, watching closely
